I Against I

Arguably one of the main revolutionary threats of the 1980s, the influence that HR has on the counter-culture is a reflection of the contradictions of a man who can be seen as a martyr and messiah of transgression.

Fueled by HR's psychic energy, the hardcore punk scene flourished while HR's state of mind languished. In those troubled moments, he sought to calm his tumultuous spirit through of communion with Jah.

In yet another transgression, HR and his companions, in addition to offering a cathartic and quintessential hardcore punk experience, give us the paradoxical and brooding reggae, with a strangely lucid HR clinging to a ray of reason

But all in vain. It was too late, HR took the path of the prophets, with his "hunting rod" which, not coincidentally, served as his acronym, later changed to "human rights". Thus, holding his hunting rod, he destroyed the desert of the real and with it the last things that connected him to the material world. And so, he abandoned the real, exchanging it for divine wisdom and the punishment of never being understood again.
